Creates a gradient object.

(x1, y1) is the location to draw with colour1. Likewise (x2, y2) is where colour2 should be. In between them there\textquotesingle{}s a gradient.

If is\+Radial is true, the colours form a circular gradient with (x1, y1) at its centre.

The alpha transparencies of the colours are used, so note that if you blend from transparent to a solid colour, the RGB of the transparent colour will become visible in parts of the gradient. e.\+g. blending from Colour\+::transparent\+Black to \mbox{\hyperlink{namespace_colours_a62eff4c03f8f8e0805bc5d4e47cf24ec}{Colours\+::white}} will produce a muddy grey colour midway, but Colour\+::transparent\+White to \mbox{\hyperlink{namespace_colours_a62eff4c03f8f8e0805bc5d4e47cf24ec}{Colours\+::white}} will be white all the way across.
